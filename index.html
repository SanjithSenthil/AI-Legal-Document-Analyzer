Use of AI

Use 1

Our AI model uses NLP (Natural Language Processing) to analyse the uploaded document sentence by sentence, provide a summary, and categorise information into the three different categories that were mentioned above: Red Flag, Yellow Flag, and Green Flag.

Use 2

The AI model was trained manually, using test data which consisted of sentences containing common clauses and keywords found in contracts; using sentences from each of our categories, we trained the model to accurately recognise and classify sentences.

Use 3

Our product also provides a detailed summary and analysis of the uploaded document, detailing key points and clauses outlined in the contract. It uses easy-to-understand language in order to ensure our user is able to fully understand exactly what they are agreeing to. The format of our output is also simple and user friendly, ensuring our users have an efficient and seamless experience.

Why Believe Us?

Your photos could be sold or used without consent

Many social media sites like Instagram have reserved the right to publicly distribute, modify, or store any images you upload to their platforms. TwitPic, an image sharing platform previously on Twitter, also reserved the right to distribute your pictures to any organisation they were affiliated with, meaning even if you delete your content, it is more than likely that your pictures still exist on the internet. In addition, TwitPic famously had a deal with World Entertainment Celebrity News Network which stated any image uploaded to their site could be sold and distributed by TwitPic without compensation or consent.

You might not be able to ever delete your account

Skype is an example of a company who does not offer users the option to delete their accounts. On their website, it ambiguously implies that there is an option to delete personal information, but it is not explicitly stated if all copies of this information are deleted from Skype servers.

Your online activity can be tracked by companies even after you close all tabs and leave their site

Almost every webpage nowadays has an option to “Accept or Reject Cookies”, and most people just click accept without even thinking about it. In particular, the terms and conditions of Facebook state that if you are logged into Facebook, Meta will track every website you visit which contains a Facebook “like” or “share” button, and tie this information to your name and email address. If you are not logged in, information on sites you visit are assigned a code and associated with your IP address.
